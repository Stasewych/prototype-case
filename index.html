The Ambition

Metroplex isn't just buying houses; they are buying data velocity.

The client came to us with a high-volume traffic funnel that was effectively breaking its own infrastructure. Their reliance on "no-code" builders like Elementor created a ceiling: ad conversions were throttled by load times.

01 — Architecture

The Cost of Convenience

In the early stages, tools like Elementor allowed for rapid growth. But as traffic scaled, these conveniences became bottlenecks. We made the strategic decision to strip away the heavy visual builders.

By re-engineering the core infrastructure on a custom WordPress theme using Vanilla JavaScript, we regained complete control over the DOM.

02 — UX Engineering

The High-Friction Paradox

Distress sales require detailed information—often up to thirty distinct data points. Conventional wisdom suggests short forms. We couldn't shorten the form, so we reduced the cognitive load.

By integrating public property APIs, the system auto-profiles the house as soon as the address is typed.

03 — Integration

Breaking Storage Limits

Users needed to upload dozens of high-resolution photos, a task that routinely crashed standard servers. Rather than upgrading to expensive enterprise storage, we engineered a custom tunnel directly to the Google Drive API.

Images are now uploaded straight to a structured drive folder, instantly generating public links that sync to the CRM.
